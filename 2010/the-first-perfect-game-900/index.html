The First Perfect Game — 900!

Posted in Raleigh Skeeball League by Farrell On July 21, 2010. 6 Comments

Well I’ll be damned.  It’s not very often in your life you get to witness huge milestones in sports:  When Wilt Chamberlain scored 100 points in a game, or in our era when Kobe Bryant scored 81 points, watching Jerry Rice score his 208th career touchdown,  seeing Cal Ripken play in his 2131st straight baseball game, etc etc.  And now we can add another milestone to that list:  Brendan “Scoop” Lloyd rolling a perfect game.

And it couldn’t have been in a more dramatic fashion — as the last roller in the 10th frame, Scoop needed a 41 to tie the opponents.  So he lined up for the hundo and let ’em rip.  9 balls later the audience couldn’t believe what they had just witnessed and people started going crazy.  Scoop had done it –rolled the first perfect game ever in league play!

It’s highly unlikely that anyone will ever be able to pull this feat off again during our lifetime, so the next time you see Scoop, shake his hand, get a picture with him, and get his autograph because this will be one story you’ll be telling for generations to come!
